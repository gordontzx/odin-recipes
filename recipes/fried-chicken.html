Fried Chicken

home

Ingredients:

  • 1 (3 to 4 pound) chicken, cut into 8 pieces
  • 2 cups buttermilk
  • 3 cups all-purpose flour, divided
  • 1 tbsp salt
  • 1 tbsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp cayenne pepper (optional, for heat)
  • 1 tbsp paprika (helps with browning)
  • Vegetable or peanut oil for frying (enough to fill a deep skillet or Dutch oven by 1/2 to 1/3)

Instructions:

  • Marinate the chicken: Place the chicken pieces in a large bowl or a resealable plastic bag. Pour the buttermilk over the chicken, cover, and refrigerate for at least 1 hour, or ideally overnight, to ensure juicy, tender meat.
  • Prepare the coating: In a large shallow dish or a robust plastic bag, combine the flour, salt, black pepper, garlic powder, onion powder, cayenne pepper, and paprika. Mix well until all seasonings are evenly distributed.
  • Coat the chicken: Remove the chicken from the buttermilk, allowing the excess to drip off. Place a few pieces at a time into the seasoned flour mixture. Seal the bag (if using) and shake well, or press the flour mixture onto the chicken in a dish, ensuring it is thoroughly coated. Transfer the coated chicken to a wire rack set over a baking sheet and let it rest for 10-15 minutes; this is a crucial step that helps the coating adhere and become crispy.
  • Heat the oil: In a deep-fat fryer, Dutch oven, or large, heavy-bottomed skillet, heat enough oil to reach an approximate temperature of 350°F (175°C). Use a kitchen thermometer for accuracy.
  • Fry the chicken: Carefully add a few pieces of chicken to the hot oil, making sure not to overcrowd the pan, as this will drop the oil temperature and result in soggy chicken. Fry in batches.
  • Dark meat (drumsticks and thighs): Fry for about 10-14 minutes, turning every few minutes, until golden brown and cooked through.
  • White meat (breasts and wings): Fry for about 6-10 minutes.
  • Check for doneness: The chicken is done when the internal temperature reaches 165°F (74°C) on a meat thermometer and the juices run clear.
  • Drain and serve: Remove the fried chicken from the oil using tongs or a slotted spoon and place it on a paper towel-lined plate or wire rack to drain the excess oil. For larger batches, you can keep the finished chicken warm in a 250°F (120°C) oven while you finish frying the rest.
